【本の内容】
Since Jeffrey Jerome Cohen edited
The Postcolonial Middle Ages
in 2000 some scholars have applied postcolonial criticism to the study of the Middle Ages. However even in postcolonial studies of Chaucer the role of non-European women in his work has not yet been fully discussed. Using postcolonial theory the author explores how Chaucer represents non-European women his Others both in gender and in culture. Her examination of non-European women in his work from a non-Westerner’s point of view reveals that his representation is complicated and ambivalent showing diverse views. The ambivalence in Chaucer reflects his own complicated position as courtier soldier minor diplomat controller of customs and poet and also the fourteenth century’s historical background and attitude.
